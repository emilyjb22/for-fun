Welome to Paper People, a genetics simulation passion project by me, Emily Brereton.

This is my first solo project. Whatever exists here, the good and the bad, is 100% my own. Conception and planning, project organization, creation of genetics logic, and all lines of code were completed by me, for me, with no outside guidance aside from what I could glean online.

This app attempts to replicate real human genetic inheritance. Users can create, randomize, and reproduce a series of computerized people. Genetic information is stored as a genotype, with a corresponding phenotype, and genetic information is passed to offspring.

At this time, users can manually select or randomize traits, and users can select two parents (male and female) to make a baby. Individuals are stored to a population.

Eventually, users will be able to randomize large numbers of individuals and statistically analyze population data over time.

Currently, individuals have hair color and eye color. I intend on adding skin color, hair texture, face shape, and other genetic attributes, as well as a GUI.

Genetic information is loosely based on real-life inheritance patterns. Traints are polygenic, alleles are varying degrees of codominant.

Project currently exists in two forms: 1) Java (furthest in development) and 2) Python (early in development).
